How does student debt vary by region?

Researcher
smart_toy Gemini Assistant

Student debt in the United States is not distributed evenly. There are significant geographical variations driven by state funding for higher education, the concentration of private versus public institutions, the local cost of living, and state-level financial aid policies.

Here is a breakdown of how student debt varies by region and the factors driving those differences.

1. The Northeast (Highest Debt)

The Northeast, particularly New England and the Mid-Atlantic, consistently ranks as the region with the highest average student debt.

  • • Key States: New Hampshire, Pennsylvania, Connecticut, and Rhode Island often top the list, with average debt per borrower frequently exceeding $35,000 to $39,000.
  • •
    Why it's high:
    • — Concentration of Private Colleges: This region has a high density of older, prestigious private non-profit universities, which generally have higher tuition than public schools.
    • — Low State Funding: States like New Hampshire and Pennsylvania historically provide some of the lowest levels of state support for higher education.
